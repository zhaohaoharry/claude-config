Research project "Climate Similarity and Supplier Choice": when a firm forms a *new* global supplier link, does the bilateral climate distance between its city and a candidate supplier city affect which city it picks? **HEADLINE SETTLED 2026-06-12 (PI-approved): MECHANISM-VERIFIED CLIMATE MATCHING — firms prefer climatically SIMILAR suppliers, and it is a genuine climate mechanism because the preference is CONCENTRATED in climate-exposed industries (ag/food/beverage/wood/paper/utilities/construction) and STATISTICALLY ABSENT for services/finance/info (clean placebo).** Industry-exposure heterogeneity (a priori NAICS binary, hemisphere interaction + formal triple interaction): matching differential slope −0.10 customer-side (t=−5.6), −0.08 supplier-side (t=−3.6), both p<0.001; services ≈0. Pooled matching −0.0178 (p=0.036). **The brief "matching-vs-hedging duality" framing (diversify on wind / cross-hemisphere) was REVERSED 2026-06-12: the hedging leg FAILED its mechanism test** — the cross-hemisphere climate-dissimilarity flip is statistically identical for services and exposed firms (`flip:HIGH` +0.036 ns cust, −0.091 ns supp), so it is NOT climate-driven (reported as a likely-mechanical seasonal-offset secondary, not hedging). Wind demoted (hump-shaped in distance, 6–10k band only; not a hemisphere effect). Resumed 2026-06-05; **2026-06-11 FULL TOP-5 PUSH** (PI mandate: build the framework, run the full battery, find motivated specs rather than forfeit on insignificance; final draft incl. appendix ≥60pp). Retitling to *Climate and the Formation of Global Supply Chains*.

Data: FactSet supply chains + Worldscope (HQ city, NAICS, financials) + GADM centroids + ERA5 monthly climate 1973–2021. Choice panel (rebuilt, time-varying): choice occasion `decision_id` (customer×industry×first-year) × candidate supplier-city-industry-year; `establish`=1 for the realized supplier's city, controls = all same-NAICS4 candidate cities. **33.85M rows, 185,390 decisions, choice mean 0.0055.** Six standardized climate-distance measures (z_): Doherty 7-BIO, Diallo normalized-7BIO, Chen–Niu annual-profile, Lamonaca temp/precip/wind 30y. Headline estimator = conditional logit via `ppmlhdfe`, absorb(`decision_id` `scity_sind_yid`[=Berry δ_jkt]), two-way cluster (`centroid` `decision_id`). The BLP/Berry `sorting_model.do` is demoted to a structural robustness.

**P0 CLEARED (2026-06-12) — identification LOCKED, the old fragility/sign worries RESOLVED.** The crux this memory previously flagged (is "prefer-similar" robust? does gravity kill it?) is settled positively: (1) **Inference** — full-sample 7-BIO survives TWO-WAY clustering at the TRUE effective N=2,371 distinct climates (not the row count): −0.0178, p=0.036 (old single-cluster SE was understated 2.5×). (2) **Country-confounder purge STRENGTHENS the effect** — an LPM identification ladder (reghdfe; ppmlhdfe IRLS stalls with country FE — see [[run-programs-background-bash]]) goes from −0.000164 (p=0.021, decision-FE only) to −0.000266 (p<0.001) adding supplier-country FE: the bilateral gravity confounders (RTA/language/colony) bias the climate coef TOWARD zero, so "it's just gravity/trade-ties" is dead. This **overturns the 2026-06-07 "full-gravity flips the sign" scare** (that was over-control on a subsample). (3) **Not a distance artifact** — among candidates within 2000km the effect is larger (−0.063, p=0.023); McFadden 30-sampled alternatives reproduce the full-set estimate. (4) Within-country (strictest) is right-signed but UNDERPOWERED (−0.0165, p=0.335; same-country pairs have little climate spread) — a POWER phenomenon, not a reversal. Wind is the placebo (≈0).

**MAGNITUDE — corrected.** ~1.8% lower choice odds per SD of climate distance (full identification). The per-SD ratio to geographic distance is **0.052 (~5%)** — a robust **SECOND-ORDER** friction, *not* the "~two-thirds of distance within-country" claimed in earlier notes (that was wrong: it mis-compared a raw climate coef to a raw distance coef without standardizing distance, and leaned on the underpowered within-country sample). Verified per-SD logit: climate 7-BIO −0.0178, standardized log-distance −0.344.

**Counterfactual REVIVED.** The earlier "warming counterfactual = rounding error" call held only for UNIFORM warming (which differences out of |C_i−C_j|). DIFFERENTIAL (lat-amplified) warming RE-ORDERS the similarity matrix — 71% of cities reshuffle their similarity ranking, 12.2% of pairs flip — so the Group F counterfactual through the identified similarity margin has real signal (CMIP6 build warranted). See [[climate-three-margins]].
