Paper proposes treating Harvested Wood Products as anthropogenic "Carbon Batteries" for carbon storage, bypassing biological saturation of forests.

**Why:** The quantitative finding (wood products can close ~13-16% of the carbon gap) could be policy-relevant at a global scale. Author aims for PNAS/Nature/Science.

**How to apply:** Key bottleneck is Section 4 (IO/substitution analysis) — without real data this remains speculative. Author needs to decide between EXIOBASE MRIO analysis vs. published displacement factors. Sections 5-6 and all figures are also needed. The Duration-Adjusted Storage metric could be the novel contribution if developed rigorously.
