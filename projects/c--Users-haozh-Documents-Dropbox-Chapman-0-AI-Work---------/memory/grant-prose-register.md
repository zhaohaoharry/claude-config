For all grant/fund applications, write in **formal academic register that is also clear and precise**. There are three registers, and only the third is acceptable:
1. **Translationese** (reject) — stiff, word-for-word, noun-stacked jargon piles (e.g. 可计算测度、测度化因果识别、逐病例可核算的知识吸收度量). Hard to understand.
2. **Colloquial/spoken** (reject) — easy to read but undignified for a proposal (e.g. 把病看好、量得准靠得住、数挂了多少牌、打交道、够得着标杆、自卖自夸、白白损失不少、跑通技术路径).
3. **Formal-but-clear academic** (use) — precise terminology, measured tone, well-formed sentences; define a technical term in-line the first time (e.g. "会聚效度（与盲评专家评分同向）") rather than dropping it raw or replacing it with slang.

**Why:** The user reviews these proposals to expert panels. Translationese reads as machine output and obscures the argument; colloquial prose reads as unserious. The funder expects the register of a strong CSSCI/SSCI paper or NSFC/社科基金 proposal.

**How to apply:** Clarity comes from short well-formed sentences, concrete subjects, and one name per concept — NOT from spoken idioms. Keep the precise academic vocabulary and gloss it; cut spoken particles, rhetorical asides, and folksy metaphors. The intuition-first / agent-verb craft from [[word-com-pdf-export-visible]]'s sibling guides still applies, but rendered in formal register. Applies to every project under 0.AI (Research, Work/基金申请, etc.).
