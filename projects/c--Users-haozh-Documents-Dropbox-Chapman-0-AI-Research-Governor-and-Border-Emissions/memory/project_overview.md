**Project:** Governor and Border Emissions
**Working title:** "When the Player Becomes the Judge: Elevated Provincial Leaders and Transboundary Pollution in China"
**Authors (alphabetic, equal contribution):** Haoyuan Ding (SUFE), Bei Ni (SHUPL), Chang Xue (SYSU), Hao Zhao (RUC/Chapman) — shared first authorship.
**GitHub/Overleaf repo:** `https://github.com/zhaohaoharry/When-the-Player-Becomes-the-Judge.git` — synced to Overleaf. Local folder: `When-the-Player-Becomes-the-Judge\` inside the project root.
**Local project root:** `c:\Users\haozh\Documents\Dropbox_Chapman\0.AI\Research\Governor and Border Emissions\`

**Research question:** Does the appointment of a provincial leader to the CPC Politburo (creating a "player-judge" with dual accountability) reduce transboundary pollution at provincial borders?

**Identification:** Generalized DiD around Politburo elevation events. Key outcome: polluting-industry activity on both sides of the provincial border. Mechanism test exploits prevailing wind direction (downwind vs. upwind neighbors).

**Why:** This is the cross-project context anchor — needed to set scope, frame coauthor communications, and avoid drifting from the paper's stated identification strategy.

**How to apply:** Treat the paper title, authorship order, and repo location as fixed. When the user says "the paper" or "the repo," they mean these. When discussing identification, default to the wind-direction mechanism test as the headline causal evidence unless told otherwise.
