  type: feedback
---

When the user explains *to me* why a choice is right (e.g. "σ should be the externality because the private cost is already in the water term; social cost would double-count"), that explanation is guidance for me, not prose for the draft. Do not paste it into the manuscript as an added sentence. Make the substantive edit it implies (here: rename "social cost" → "external cost"), and stop there.

**Why:** The reader needs the corrected statement, not the author's internal justification for it. Inserting the rationale reads as a research-note / decision meta-sentence. Related: [[feedback_no_author_decision_meta]], [[feedback_no_research_note_language]], [[feedback_no_decision_sentences]].

**How to apply:** Treat reasoning the user gives me as a spec, not copy. Apply the implied change; never quote the reasoning in the draft. Applies across all research projects.
