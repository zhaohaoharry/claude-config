When a paragraph reports two or three key empirical findings from a figure or table, use the following structure:

1. **Setup sentence:** name the variation and announce how many findings follow. *"Varying $\sigma$ yields two key conclusions."* / *"Three patterns stand out across the eight scenarios."* / *"The decomposition delivers two results."*
2. **Each finding as one declarative sentence:** *"First, [headline conclusion with the number]. Second, [headline conclusion with the number]."* The conclusion sentence carries the substantive claim plus the threshold/number; no preamble.
3. **Closing intuition sentence:** one sentence that ties the findings to an economic mechanism. *"This pattern reflects the tradeoff between X and Y."* / *"The threshold corresponds to the point where the externality penalty offsets the reallocation rent."*

**Why:** This is how AER/QJE results paragraphs read. Each sentence carries either a substantive finding or a mechanism interpretation. Nothing is spent telegraphing the paragraph's own structure.

**Bad (telegraphs structure, defers substance):**
> *"Where the ranking does change with $\sigma$, the empirically interesting crossovers cluster in two bands. Centralized $\times$ tax remains the global maximum at every $\sigma$ above \$420/AF, the lower edge of the literature-defensible envelope, and below that threshold centralized $\times$ open access takes the lead on the strength of its \$0.54 billion static reallocation rent. The three open-access pairwise crossovers cluster near the \$1{,}000/AF urban-water benchmark. [details of three crossover prices...]. The ranking among the three open-access cells is therefore empirically contested at the policy-relevant externality value, exactly as the second-best logic predicts."*

The opening *"the crossovers cluster in two bands"* tells the reader the prose structure rather than the finding. The reader has to read three sentences before learning what wins where.

**Good (states the findings directly):**
> *"Varying $\sigma$ yields two key conclusions. First, centralized $\times$ tax outperforms all other regimes whenever $\sigma$ exceeds \$420/AF, the conservative lower bound of the literature-defensible range. Second, the open-access pairwise crossovers cluster near the \$1{,}000/AF urban-water benchmark, with autarky dominating at high $\sigma$ and the centralized market winning at low $\sigma$. This pattern reflects the tradeoff between surface-water market efficiency and the negative externality from groundwater leakage."*

Four sentences. Reader knows the answer by sentence 2. Sentence 4 supplies the economic intuition that earns the finding its weight.

**Apply this style** to any results / sensitivity / decomposition paragraph that has two or three discrete findings. It composes with [[feedback-no-research-note-language]] (don't telegraph structure) and [[feedback-topic-sentence-first]] (topic sentence carries the substantive claim).

Applies across all research projects.
