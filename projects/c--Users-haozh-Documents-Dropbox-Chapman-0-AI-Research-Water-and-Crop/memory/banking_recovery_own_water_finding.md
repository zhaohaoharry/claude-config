INVESTIGATED 2026-07-02 (4-track: board-minutes PDFs, code, web, quant). The big "Recovery
(banking pull)" rows in the 2014-15 SWA tables (189k AF, 25-58% of the three no-GW districts'
documented supply) are recovery of the districts'/landowners' OWN previously-banked water from
Kern Fan programs (Pioneer + Berrenda Mesa Spreading Grounds; Wonderful via Westside Mutual owns
48% of Kern Water Bank) at cost-of-service fees $27-$102/AF. Purchases of others' banked water
are booked separately at market prices (BVWSD 21k AF @ $800 in Pioneer, Nov 2015). Full memo
with quotes + sources: quality_reports/banking_recovery_investigation_20260702.md.

**Why it matters:** the model has NO banking state; it books ALL of the 213k AF 2014-15 buyer
deficit as contemporaneous seller pump-and-sell. The banking cycle (recharge wet, recover dry,
~10% haircut) contributes ~0 to the true baseline-vs-autarky pumping delta, so the 3.25 MAF /
77% leakage attribution is likely overstated in direction; magnitude unquantified (pro-rata
B132 smear already embeds recovery county-wide). Sell-and-pump mechanism itself is documented
and survives (pool/spot/banked-water sales at $600-$1,300/AF margins). Manuscript line ~305
"no itemized public record" is factually wrong (265k AF recovery+pool itemized > 213k deficit).
