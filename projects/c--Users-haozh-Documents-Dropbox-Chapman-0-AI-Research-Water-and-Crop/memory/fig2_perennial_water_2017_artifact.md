Water and Crop manuscript, Figure 2 (`fig:sf1`). Redesigned 2026-06-16 from a two-panel (crop-share + SWP-allocation) figure into a single dual-axis figure: left axis = total perennial crop **acreage** in the 24 ag districts (1,000 acres, line), right axis = total surface water delivered to the same districts (million AF, bars). Generated by `program/sandbox/draw_fig_sf1_v2.py` → `latex/figures/sf1_perennial_water.pdf`. Series: perennial acreage 308k (1998) → 595k (2022); ag-district SW fell ~42% (1.71 MAF 1998–2005 mean → 0.99 MAF 2012–2022 mean), so manuscript prose updated 43%→42%.

**Why acreage, not the perennial share:** the share has a spurious one-year spike to 61.3% in 2017 (vs 52.4% in 2016, 53.8% in 2018). It is a pure denominator artifact — total mapped farmland drops to 893k acres in 2017 vs ~1.1M in adjacent years. Perennial acreage itself is smooth across 2016–2018 (578k → 547k → 590k). Plotting acreage removes the artifact entirely. The Fact-1 prose quotes clean endpoint shares (36% in 1997, 59% in 2022), neither contaminated.

**Root cause of the 2017 raw-panel dip (diagnosed 2026-06-16):** the gap lives only in the RAW permit/plot panel (`crop_plot_owner_WD.dta`), not the estimation panel. In 2017 it maps ~2,600 fewer plots (10,457 vs 13,060/13,413) and ~210k fewer acres. The loss is overwhelmingly ANNUAL row crops — vegetables −82k, cotton/other-field −37k, grain −34k — while perennials barely move (almonds −20k, others <−9k). It is broad across districts (heaviest in the big annual districts: Kern Delta −35%, Arvin-Edison −28%, Semitropic −19%), NOT one district dropping out. It is NOT real fallowing: fallow acreage actually FELL in 2017 (88k vs ~97k), so the parcels are absent from the 2017 crop-map vintage, not idled. The pixel-reconstructed panel (`mle_sample_v2.dta`) keeps the dot COUNT flat (43,404 in 2017 vs 43,556/43,562) because the grid is fixed — BUT it does NOT repair the crop labels. The reconstruction rule labels an unmapped pixel (no crop in the year's map, no standing orchard) as FALLOW, not carry-forward. So in the pixel panel fallow RISES in 2017 (25.9%→29.0%, +1,310 dots) and annual drops (21.8%→19.4%). [CORRECTION: an earlier note here wrongly said the pixel panel "fully repairs" the gap and estimation is "unaffected" — it is not; the 2017 fallow bump propagates into estimation.]

Recovery is partial: of 1,382 dots Annual(2016)→Fallow(2017), only 42% return to a crop in 2018 (31% annual, 11% perennial); 58% stay fallow (partly genuine secular annual decline). The clearly-spurious subset = 575 dots cropped-2016 / fallow-2017 / cropped-2018 (~1.3% of dots). So ~1,382 spurious annual→fallow transitions enter estimation in a WET (non-drought) year, mildly working against Fact 2. Proposed fixes (not yet applied, awaiting user): (1) carry-forward/interpolation fill of the bracketed 575; (2) drop 2017 transitions; (3) leave + robustness footnote. Note: Figure 2 itself is unaffected (perennial line barely dips).

**Corroboration (it is NOT real fallowing):** 2017 was the WETTEST year in the sample — highest surface water (2.98 MAF) and SWP allocation 85%, the record-snowpack year that ended the 2012–2016 drought (drought = 2014 SWP 5%, 2015 20%). In a wet year annuals should expand, not idle, so a real 210k-acre annual collapse is economically nonsensical. Also ruled out a classification/spelling gap: unmapped (cat==0) rows are tiny and stable (~101–125/yr) and 2017 has MORE distinct crop names (155) than 2016 (138); the raw row count is genuinely ~20% lower (10,960 vs ~14,000). Source vintage is simply thinner.
