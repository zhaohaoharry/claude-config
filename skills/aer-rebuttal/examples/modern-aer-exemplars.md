# Modern AER Exemplars — by Subfield

A subfield-organized reference of **recent (2018-2025) top-5 economics
papers** with public replication packages. Curated specifically for
*architecture + estimator* alignment with the AER-skills stack: modern
DiD (Callaway-Sant'Anna), weak-IV-robust inference, Calonico-Cattaneo
RDD, Borusyak-Hull-Jaravel shift-share, AEA-compliant deposits.

Read this alongside [`aer-exemplars.md`](aer-exemplars.md), which
covers the older canonical architecture references.

**How to use.** Pick the 2-3 most relevant papers for your subfield +
identification strategy, reverse-outline each in 30 minutes (intro
architecture / identification one-liner / robustness battery), then
draft your own paper against those references.

## Reading Strategy

The most efficient way to use this file is the **3-paper match**:

1. **One subfield architecture exemplar.** Pick the paper closest to your
   topic in your subfield section above. Read for introduction
   architecture, not estimator.
2. **One identification exemplar.** Pick a paper using your identification
   strategy from a *different* subfield. Read for the identification
   defense paragraph.
3. **One deposit exemplar.** Pick any post-2020 openICPSR project from
   the AEJ Best Papers list. Open the README and the master script;
   copy the structure for your own deposit.

Total time: 90 minutes. Output: a written architecture sketch you can
use to draft your own intro against.

## Caveats

1. **Estimator vs. architecture.** Many older AER papers (e.g., Card-
   Krueger 1994, AJR 2001) remain *architecturally* canonical even
   though their estimators are now superseded. Read older papers for
   structure; read post-2020 papers for current estimator practice.
2. **Subfield boundaries are fuzzy.** Several papers below appear under
   multiple subfields because they have cross-subfield reach — that is
   precisely why they succeeded at AER.
3. **Deposit quality varies.** Older openICPSR projects (pre-2020) may
   not satisfy the current AEA Data Editor standard. Trust 2021+
   deposits as templates; treat earlier ones as reference for *what to
   include*, not *how to format*.
4. **Field experiments.** Any field-experiment paper should also be
   registered in the [AEA RCT Registry](https://www.socialscienceregistry.org/);
   the AEJ-Applied and AER policy require it.
